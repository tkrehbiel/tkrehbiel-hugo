Elmer's WW1 Diaries

Co. H, 316th Infantry · American Expeditionary Forces · 1918–1919

This site presents the wartime diaries and letters of Elmer H. Krehbiel, who served with Company H, 316th Infantry, in the American Expeditionary Forces in France during the First World War. Each entry below links to a single page, showing an image of the original alongside a transcription of Elmer's handwriting.

Elmer's Journey & Major Milestones

April 28, 1918

Elmer is drafted into the Army and reports to Camp Meade, Maryland, for basic training.

July 7, 1918

Boards the USS Agamemnon for France, arriving at the port of Brest on July 19.

September 26, 1918

The Meuse-Argonne Offensive near Montfaucon.

September 30, 1918

Wounded by a shell fragment and subsequently hospitalized for a month at Base Hospital 23 in Vittel.

November 1, 1918

Returning to the Front in the Grande Montagne sector.

November 5, 1918

Narrowly avoiding the devastating battle of Hill 378 ("Hill of Death"), which decimates his company.

November 7, 1918

Gassed while sleeping in a shellhole.

November 11, 1918

The Armistice is signed while Elmer is hospitalized in Souilly, recovering from gas and pneumonia.

January 17, 1919

Rejoins his company in Issoncourt.

February 23, 1919

Attending VB school for weapons practice.

March 4, 1919

Teaching an evening English class.

May 16, 1919

Embarks on the USS Texan from St. Nazaire to return home, docking in Philadelphia on May 29.

June 6, 1919

Demobilized at Camp Dix, New Jersey.

The diaries and letters on this site were photographed/scanned and re-transcribed in 2026 by Thomas Krehbiel. The static site generator to create this site was built by prompting Claude AI and Antigravity.
