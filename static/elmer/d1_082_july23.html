At the rate we traveled
today, we won’t reach our
destination till the war
is over. We would go a
mile and then stop, then
another mile and stop. At
noon we held up the brake-
man and showed him
a question in our French-
English book, “What is the
name of this station.” He
wrote out on the car “Mennetou”(?)
and added, “Vierzon(?), 16 kilos.”
Vierzon(?) is a big railroad
center. We reached Vierzon
at night.
