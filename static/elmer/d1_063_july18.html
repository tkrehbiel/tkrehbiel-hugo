but it was not until 1.30
that the first signs be-
came visible. Then a
lighthouse, and behind it
a low green bank became
visible. We also spotted an
observation balloon, a tiny
speck in the sky. We drew
steadily in and at 3.30
passed through between
two high cliffs, clothed
with verdure(?), into a land-
locked harbor. Here we came
to anchor.

We will have to spend
another night on board.
Four companies go off yet
tonight, but ours is not
one of the fortunate ones.

I missed dinner because
I forgot to set my watch
