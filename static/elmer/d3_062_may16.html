the battalion and marched to
La Pallet. We reached there
at noon. There we lay around
till 5 oclock when our train
pulled in. We promptly entrained
and were pulled off, to Nantes,
then to St. Nazaire.

We got off on the docks.
A great ship was lying along-
side, bright with lights, and
with a band playing. Soldiers
were boarding her, and we
thought we would go on too.
But instead we were marched
off about 5 kilos, a fast, hot
hike. We arrived at the em-
barkation camp, a village of
huts big enough to hold a
division. We had supper, then
were turned out again for our
inspection.

It was past 12 oclock
when we got into our bunks. And
we only got a few hours sleep at
that.

Friday May 16

At 5 oclock we had
breakfast, and soon
after were on the move again
