At Avocourt there was
a gathering place of the walk-
ing wounded. Here I was
given a seat near(?) a fire,
and the seventh meal. Soon,
a big truck drew up. 10 of
us were loaded onto it. We
rode for about an hour and
a half, along a bum road
lined with guns and stacks
of ammunition that had not
yet been moved forward. We
pulled up, after dusk, at
a tent hospital. Here our
records were taken, we were
