Saturday Nov 2

I soon got back
to the company. And
the lieutenant told me I could-
n’t be taken back without a
duty certificate from the doctor!
so I had to walk back to
town; and I spent another night there.

Sunday Nov 3

I got back to the
company. The lieut.
excused me from duty for four
or five days, so I hunted(?) up
a dugout, opened my pack and
made myself comfortable.

Monday Nov 4

This woods is certainly
a warm place! Shells are
dropping all the time, some-
times singly, sometimes in
regular hailstorms(?). Most of the
company are quartered in Nisson
huts, which they inherited from
the Germans. Of course, the Huns
know just where they are and
every once in a while they send
a bunch of shells over at them.
So far they have not hit, but, as
one fellow said, “That hut of ours
did everything but turn around
