We stayed down there for three hours and
sweated, before they allowed us up on deck.
That was a bit more bearable, for we could
watch the work around the ship, and we
had before us a view that has(?) been(?) photo-
graphed and magazined(?) 19,361,482 times.
It did look exactly like the pictures, we could
easily pick out the principal points. I was
sorry though, that we didn’t get leave; but
they knew what they were about; half of that
bunch would have lost their way and not
been able to get back.

The ship is an interesting animal. As
it lay there at the dock (it was one of the
largest(?) transports in the service) it looked
as solid(?) as the(?) steel(?) and(?) concrete(?) dock
itself. One would just as soon have expected
the Capitol to rock and pitch. But there
was one thing that I noticed: when we
went aboard the gangplanks were sloping
at about a 30° angle; when the last troops
came aboard they were very close to
level. This boat carries mostly livestock(?).
