Letter from Elmer, June 26, 1918 — Page 3

The lower half of my tent was down,
and the blankets, and my legs, to my
knees were soaked. Worse yet, my rifle
was wet. I wiped that off a little, then
crawled out, it was still raining, and
re pitched the tent. Then I got back in,
curled up on a little dry spot and waited
for daylight. Reveille came at last and
I got up. I found water standing in
my shoes, and my leggins [sic] were soaked
and as stiff as boards. I finally got
dressed(?) and crawled out.

I had thought I was bad off, but
I looked around and then considered my-
self lucky. About half the tents had
standing water in them, seven or eight
were entirely blown down; some of the
fellows had been laying in water half
the night. We all felt pretty miserable,
but took it the best we could. The prize(?)
joke was on a couple of fellows up above
