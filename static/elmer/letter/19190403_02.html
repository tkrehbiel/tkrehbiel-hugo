Mine was pretty heavy, for I had

We left town at about 9.45.
We fell in at 8.30, but of course
we had to(?) stand(?) around(?) with our
packs on for a while. The march
for the first two hours was not
bad. The road was good, and
the pace easy and after we caught
our second wind we didn’t mind
the pack. But about 12 oclock it
began to sleet; just little flurries
of dry hard hail. It was an-
noying, but not very bad.

We had sent out a billeting
detail to find bunks(?) for us at the
end of each day. We reached the
place at about 2.00, in a little
town whose name I have forgotten,
just below Conde(?). Troops(?), of
our own division had been sta-
