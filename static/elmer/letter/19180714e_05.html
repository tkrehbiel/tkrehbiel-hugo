I didn’t eat supper, or breakfast in the
morning, I expected to be sick, so I didn’t
put(?) to waste the jawpower. But, perhaps
because of this, I didn’t mind it at all
after the first feeling of drunkenness. But
I did eat a big dinner. The feeding here
was a revelation to me. You have your
food dished out to you on the run, then
you stand at tables, boards about 15” wide,
hung from the ceiling by chains. You begin
at the front end of the table and, as the
places are vacated beside you, you slide your
plates down toward the end, so that you wind
up at the [?] end. Then you walk(?) [?]
long trough full of [redacted], in which you
dip your dishes, then go on deck and scrape
them out and polish them with your shirt tail
