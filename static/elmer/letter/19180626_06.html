Letter from Elmer, June 26, 1918 — Page 6

P.S. If you’ve got any white woolen, or
strong cotton, rags in there I wish you’d
send them to me. I need them for cleaning
my gun. Anything will do, just so its strong
I dont want anything that rips easily.

Elmer

I wrote the above about 3 hours ago.
Since that time we have been hurriedly called
in off the range; we did the 8 1/2 miles in
1 hour and 10 minutes. There is a rumor that
we will very shortly move; some of the guard
overheard the colonel talking about. I know
for certain that the sergeant and lieutenant
that are to precede the company will go off
tomorrow. So it looks like we are going to pull
out. I’ll make a strenuous effort to get a
pass Saturday; try to get the money to me.

[The rest of the page is blank.]
