finished we went to the mess sergeant (it
was the kitchen coal pile) and demanded
something to eat. So he promised us a
special meal after the other wards had
been fed. We waited, perhaps not many
of us expecting very much. But the mess
sergeant came after us, led us to the
mess hall and told us to be seated. Then
he actually sent in some K.P’s to wait
on us and such a meal! It began with
a surloin [sic] steak that was actually too
big to go into(?) [illegible] hot(?), and smothered(?)
with onions. Then macaroni and cheese,
potatoes and gravy and bread, butter and
coffee, with cream and sugar. We each
had enough for three men. I ate all I
could, then loaded my mess kit so full
that I couldn’t get the lid on. I’m
going to eat it as soon as I have room
again. But I sure will remember that
meal as long as I live.
