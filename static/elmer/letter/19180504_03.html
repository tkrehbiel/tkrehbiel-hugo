My feet generally complain. My shoes are entirely busted
up, I doubt if they’ll last till next Monday. But
I’ve got Kitchen Police for tomorrow and no drill Sunday,
maybe they’ll last.

There is nothing to complain of [–] in the regular
work. I sometimes wish though that I might sift out
some of the men in our barracks. It isn’t that they’re
bad fellows, they have [–] the wrong spirit, or no spirit at
all. And the slackness of any one man reflects on the
whole roomful. But in about a week there’ll be a
general sorting out and shifting around, when we are
assigned our permanent places in the army.

I have written home twice now and I havn’t [sic]
had a reply yet. I havn’t [sic] got my address check yet
from school; did you get my address to Mr. Cham-
berlain? And I’ve said twice that I need that
paper with the [–] two holes punched at the top.
