They give us strenuously physical exercises for
half an hour before drill and half an hour at the
end. I enjoy those before drill; I do not enjoy those
after drill. A specialty here is breathing exercises. My
lungs have pumped more wind here in a week than they
did before in a month. I’ve managed to straighen [sic]
myself out but even then my chest feels strained; there
isn’t enough room. They have ordered a 39” blouse for
me, I guess that they will see that I fill it.

Drill is over at 11 o’clock and we have a couple of
hours in barracks, and dinner. The evening drill is
over at 4.30, and that practically ends our days work.
We fall in ranks for retreat at 6.00. Bedtime is 10.00,
so we have plenty of time for moving around the camp.
We generally go down to the Y.M.C.A., where there is
singing and movies and so on. There is also a
regular theatre [sic] and picture theater [sic] here, but I’m
totally broke.

We have been measured for our uniforms, but we
wont get them till the first of next week. We were
also given our physical examination. I and about
fifteen others were marked down with bad hearts. But
they didn’t say what they were going to do with us
and they dont let up on drill for us; I suppose if

[Written vertically up the left side of the page:]

Send me a small calendar, we’re worse off than Robinson Crusoe out here.
