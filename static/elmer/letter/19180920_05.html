climate. I used to watch the big wagon-
loads of hay being pulled into the barns
at our first town and I’d always hope
we’d have it that way some day. The stuff
makes beautiful hay, a bright green, with
a very sweet smell. Its no wonder the
stock looks so good. They use it for a
soiling crop too, and a little patch about
50 by 100 feet will keep a cow all summer, for
I’ve seen it two feet high at one end before it
was all cut at the other. It is cut and
hauled to the barns in wheelbarrows, twice
a day.

Say, when you build anything, make
it solid. If I come back, that is one good
thing that I will have gotten out of this trip.
These French villages are certainly busted up,
but I always wonder, as I pass through one,
how an average American village would look
after such a pounding with big guns. I bet
it would even erase the name of it from the
map. Here one big shell can hit a house
