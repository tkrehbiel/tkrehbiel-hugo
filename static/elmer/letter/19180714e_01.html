[A badly faded letter.]

Ocean View
Somewhere in July.

[Estimated around July 14th based on the events mentioned, and correlating with the diary entries.]

Dear folks.

I cant think what to write about,
I am all at sea. (clever, aint it) But it is
a puzzle though, for we are now within the
dominion of the Censor. They say one fellow
wrote thirteen pages without putting in a word
that had to be scratched out. I wont contest
his championship.

We left Camp Meade shortly; very
shortly, after I wrote my last letter. We marched
to the train and promptly pulled out. When
we arrived at the port of embarkation we went
directly to the transport. No we didn’t either;
we stood around with heavy packs on our
backs for several hours. Then we went aboard.
As soon as I saw our quarters I wished we
had stood around on the dock till ten minutes
before we sailed. Our bunks were two feet
wide and five feet long, in three tiers in a room
where I could reach up and touch the palm(?)
