make cement grenades, and I think the methods
we used could be applied to cement drain
pipes. If I go back to the shop(?) I’ll make
a set of molds, and you can turn out enough
pipe to drain all those little runs on our
place. I’ve seen some pretty farms over here
and I hope to get back with ambition enough
left to make ours likewise. So you can invest(?)
that money I’m sending home and put it
away for me or you can spend it. I th??
I can send home 25 dollars a month, because
we are now way beyond the shops and there
is nothing to spend money on. I’m glad I
dont smoke; it is practically a soldiers only expense.

If you want to inquire about my insurance
ask about Cpl. Elmer H Krehbiel, 2710772
Co. H, 316th Inf. U.S.A.
I thought you knew that I insured for $10,000, in
your favor.

My address is the same as usual. I men-
tion it because you may notice that there is a
change in the upper left hand cor-
ner of the envelope.

Elmer.

O.K. Censored
[no signature]
1st. Lieut 316th Inf.

[Written vertically up the left side of the page:]

P.S. There is a newspaper printed in Paris for the A.E.F., called the Stars and Stripes.
I’ll have it sent to you as soon as I get enough money to pay the subscription.
