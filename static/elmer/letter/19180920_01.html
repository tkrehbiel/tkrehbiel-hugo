Cpl. Elmer H. Krehbiel
Co. H, 316th Inf. U.S.A.
Am. E. F.

Friday, Sept. 20, ’18

Dear folks.

I just received two letters from
home and will write immediately. This
is the third time this week that I have
written you. The kids want to know [–] where
I am. I am living in a dugout, as I des-
cribed in my last letter. But you might get
this letter before you get that one, so you’ll
just have to imagine it till he arrives it
arrives. Paper is too scarce to allow me
to repeat; I am writing this on a few sheets
that I happened to bring from Camp Meade.

The kids seem to [–] be doing
a lot of fishing. We did some fishing here
that you’ve never done. We took one of our
hand grenades and dropped it into a creek.
It went off, of course, and stunned all
the fish; we got out half a dozen six and
seven inches long. It is the only way we
can get these French fish, for they wont
