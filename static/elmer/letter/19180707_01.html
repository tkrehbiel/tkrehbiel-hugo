Camp Meade, Md.
July 7, 18

Dear folks.

You notice that my letter is
still postmarked “Camp Meade,” but I know
for certain that this is the last one. At
noon today the first sergeant said, “Anyone
who wishes to change some American money
for [–] French can do so at the Y.M.C.A.,”
and the mess sergeant said, “Supper at
4.45 tonight.” So it does not appear that
we are going to Italy.

We are all glad that the sus-
pense and worry is over. We have been
having daily inspections, of one sort or
another, for a week; we have not been allowed
outside the barracks; we have been sleeping
on the bare springs of our cots and everything
is upset generally. We have just been
laying around barracks all week; no drills
at all; except some rehersing [sic] of the way
we will get on the ship.
