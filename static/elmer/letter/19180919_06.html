by shell fire. The woods is full of shrapnel
shells that have been shot over. Bullets
are laying everywhere, French, German and
American. We even find belts of bullets for
machine guns, and cartridges for field guns,
which have never been fired, are very common.
Some Frenchman, after the war, will make
a respectable sum of money picking up the
junk in this woods. There must be a ton or
two of brass shell [?] alone.

As I said, I am very well off. The only
thing we lack now(?) is(?) water. There is plenty
to drink but(?) [?] to(?) [?] in. I havn’t [sic]
had a bath for three weeks. But it is too
cold now to undress at night, so I cant
see how dirty I am, which is some consolation.
There are no lice; but the rats have not been
exaggerated, one fellow swears one tried to
pull his blanket off last night. Also I can
already bless Esther for that sweater; it is
a necessity already. You’d better see that
Jake is equipped with a whole set when
he leaves for camp.

I’ve gotten 3 letters, and I’ve read each
one 30 times; send me another one.

Elmer.

O.K. Censored
[F. D. Johnson signature]
1st Lieut 316th Inf

[Written sideway up the left side of the page:]

I’m sending home all the money I can. Get my bond paid for, and if
possible, get another one for(?) me after that is paid for.
