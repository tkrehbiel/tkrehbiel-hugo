The weather has at last turned
cold. The days are clear now and,
joy of joys, the mud is frozen solid.
We are walking on hard ground for
the first time since August. But there
is a wind just now that goes right
through overcoat, jerkin, blouse, sweater,
shirt, undershirt and skin. And when
we have maneuvers, and are simulat-
[ing?] battle conditions it sure is tough
[illegible, torn] [to have?] to(?) lay on that hard, cold
ground. Another discomfort is that
we have to go to bed about 4.30, be-
cause even our four stoves cant keep
our barn warm now.

Say, in your next letter, let me
know if I havn’t [sic] got some relations
in Italy. We have lots of Italians
in the company and they are getting
three weeks furlough, with every expense
paid, to visit their homes.

Elmer
