our house and barn in. We have long boxes,
the bottom made of woven wire; filled with
straw, for our benches. There are plenty of
blankets, so we are warm at night and four
stoves that we keep going in the daytime. The
company is feeding good now too, we got all we
can eat. And the Y.M.C.A. has a big canteen
here, we can buy practically anything. By the
way, we can buy a can of jam, any kind,
about as large as a can of corn, for about 32
cents. And we all have paybooks, so noone is
broke any more.

We drill about four hours a day, in the
morning. In the afternoon we have games(?).
Reveille is at about 7.15 and retreat at 4.00
so we certainly dont have much work. And after
drill we can go to the YM and read, for they
have beaucoup magazines. So it seems that
the war was responsible for most of that
rough treatment we got, and the powers-that-be
really do what they can for us.

This is all the paper I have so I must quit.
I am well and happy and(?) anxious to get home.
I got my Christmas package and promptly
chewed a filling out of my tooth with that velatis(?).
Thanks for the handkerchief and that fruit cake was
great. Send more packages if you can. I got a letter from
Esther dated Dec 20. I’ll write again when
I get more paper.

Elmer

OK Censored.

[Research note: Velatis is a famous Washington, D.C. confectioner best known for its chewy caramels.]
