Aug 25 1918
Same Old Place.

Dear folks.

I got hold of some perfectly good
writing paper, so I’ll start another letter on its
way. I hope you are receiving my letters more
regularly than I am receiving yours.

This is Sunday morning, so, outside(?)
of inspection, and physical examination, and
turning in some equipment and washing our clothes
and so on, I have nothing to do. I find that it
is getting harder and harder to take up a pen and
write a letter. We are so thoroughly settled down
that everything now is commonplace, we wouldn’t
think of telling the folks at home about it, so
it seems that there is nothing to say.

I read, before I came over, of how our
soldiers here are quartered in villages, and
march every morning and evening to the drill
grounds, but I didn’t realize what it meant.
This is what it means to us. We get up at 5 o’clock,
stand reveille, then get our mess. At 6.30 we
have to fall in and march to our drill grounds.
