Camp Meade, Md.
June 13, ’18

Dear folks.

Maybe you think that my letters
are getting few and far between, but I’m
beginning to think that you’ve forgotten
me altogether.

We are now down to a regular
soldier life. All our new equipment
has been issued, including our new Enfield
rifles. In the morning we have squad
drill and sometimes extended order drill.
In the evening we have bayonet exercise
and firing exercise, tent pitching, packing
and so on; mostly bayonet practise [sic]. We
are all getting along well and all the officers
are working strenuously to have us per-
manently assigned to Co. H. At present
we are still only attached, and the future
is very uncertain.
