Also, you speak of Teddy swimming around
in Addison’s pond on his new waterwings. He
ought to see our swimming pond. It’s twenty
feet wide, twelve feet deep and two hundred
miles long; in other words, a canal. I think,
if I get back again, and get rich and want
a vacation, I’ll come back over here and
go swimming in that canal. After a days
drilling and sweating and swallowing dust
its heavenly to get into that cold water. I
am now able to swim about 100 yards and
can dive fairly well. But I show up very
poorly alongside of some of these Philadel-
phia wharf rats. And we have a Lieutenant
who certainly is a crack swimmer.

I’m glad you sent me Norman’s Address,
I’ve been wanting to write to him but I had
forgotten his address. I havn’t [sic] been able
to find Nevers on the map; I suppose its
just a little one house town like this. But
I’d like to see Norman; it’d be like meeting
in a desert.
