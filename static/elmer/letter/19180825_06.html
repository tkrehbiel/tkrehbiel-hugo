my money here, because I just spend it
foolishly. I cant for the life of me realize I’m
paying out money when I hand out one of these
shinplasters [sic]. A French bank note is blue,
and about the shape of a piece of toilet(?) paper.
They have smaller notes also; a 1 franc note
is only about 1 1/2 by 3 inches. But to make up
for that they make their copper pennies very
large. A piece worth two cents is about 1 1/2 inch-
es in diameter, larger than a silver dollar.

[“Shinplaster” — slang for a small-denomination paper note; a flimsy-looking piece of paper money.]

As I said, send the paper and the mag-
azines, especially the Movie magazine, and
send them to Norman too. The only mag-
azines we see here are British and French,
and we very seldom see any of those. You
might, if you cant send the whole paper, cut
out the “Keeping Up With the Joneses,” and
“Mutt and Jeff” for me. And write at least
once a week; you ought to be able to write two
or three, there are so many of you. Dont
always expect me to answer, because I may
not be able to. I will close now because I’ll
have to go on a practise [sic] patrol tonight and
it is nearly time. I’ll try to write twice
a week.

Elmer.

O.K. Censored
[CC Cunningham]
1st Lieut. 316th Inf.

[It looks to my untrained eye like Elmer writes in the “OK Censored” and “1st Lieut. 316th Inf.” and the lieutenant just signs in the middle.]
