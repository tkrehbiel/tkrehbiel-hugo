incident; we suddenly found our convoy increased
by one destroyer, and none of the lookouts had
reported its appearance. They got a fine bawling
out.

On the morning of what we knew would
be the last day everyone stood on deck. We
didn’t expect to see land till noon, but we
began looking for it at 8 oclock. We had dinner
at twelve, then came right up again. But it
was not till 1.30 that the first sight of land was seen.
Then we made out a tiny white mark above the
horizon. It was a lighthouse. At the same
time we spotted what we took to be an aero-
plane, but found learned later was a kite balloon. Soon
a low bank became visible on the left, and
later on the right. The [redacted] formed in
single file and we steamed in to the harbor.
The bank of land, at first grey, soon became
green, and finally we made out trees and
bushes and houses. You cant imagine how
good they looked after that long sea trip. The
