don’t get those 20 themes in before the last week of
May I wont get credit for the year’s work in English
and I’ll have to [–] take it over again. And dont
forget to get me the address of the G. W. U., its
20 something G st. N W. I couldn’t find a Washington
directory in camp. Get that paper here, that’s what
concerns me most. I ought to be writing two or three
themes every evening and here I’ve lost almost a
week already. It is lined sheets, 8 x 10 1/2 inches, with
two holes punched at the top. I brought home a roll
from school and left it lying around the house somewhere.

There’s still no chance for me to get to town;
it may be two or three weeks yet before I come in.
So you’ll have to write. If there’s nothing to write
about you can send me a picture post card at least,
just to let me know that the mails are running.
Are you going to send me a Literary Digest every week?
There is noplace around camp where I can buy one,
and I’m too far broke anyway. I’ll even have to
bum a stamp to mail a letter that I wrote on free
paper and sealed in a free envelope.

Shall I send my mail to 1016 9th or the Pension
office? I suppose you’ll go out in the country soon.

My of-
ficial
address.

Private Elmer H. Krehbiel
Attached Co. H, 316th Inf.
Camp Meade, M.D. Md.

[Written vertically up the left side of the page:]

Don’t forget a small note book, I want to keep a diary.
