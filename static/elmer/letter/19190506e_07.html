We dont know yet when we will
sail. We hear that the 313th is
loading today. We ourselves have
been de cootieized [sic] and have been sup-
plied with necessary clothes. We hear
that we are to leave here Sunday but
it’s only rumor, we dont know if its
true. Anyhow, it wont be long, so
you can prepare yourselves, and
Esther and Milton can prepare to pay
my way for the rest of the summer.
I’m going to mark myself “Light Duty”
when I get out of this army.

Elmer.

This is probably my last letter.
I suppose you’ve already stopped
writing, because, judging from other
fellow’s mail, the home folks know
pretty well when we will get there.

[Assuming this letter is misdated and was sent in May, this is the last letter Elmer sent.]
