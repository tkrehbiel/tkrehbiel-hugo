some good pamphlets of French English, issued by
the YMCA and we got one of the train crew to read
them. We found that he was more successful in
pronouncing English than we were in pronouncing

We made such poor time that it was midnight
before we arrived at the second “coffee junction.” I got
out of my bunk to get my coffee and someone else
slipped in. So I spent the rest of the night on
a bench.

To be continued(?) when(?) I get some(?) paper.

My address is now just what you see on the
upper corner of the envelope. As soon as possible I
will arrange that you draw most of my pay over there.
Two dollars a week will keep me, for milk and nuts
and figs are about all we can buy. But you might
send me some candy, or raisins or something like
that about every other week, or oftener. Our mess is not
good at all, but I expect it will improve when we are
settled. I believe you have a French-English vocab-
ulary there; send it over if it has the pronunciations(?)
in it; if not it is useless; throw it away. And
for heaven’s sake, send me the Literary Digest and
a Movie magazine. My brain is dead now, I’m
arranging(?) to leave it behind when I go to the front.
It is a fact though that we havn’t [sic] had a word of
news for a week. But we manufacture our own news
for there are 12,468,619 rumors floating around.
Write often, and enclose some newspaper clippings.

Your son (brother)
Elmer.

P.S. If When you send that candy wrap it in a good
gun(?) rag. Keeping a gun clean in this climate is a job
for a day laborer; which is just what we are now.
Send me a few dollars; there’s no telling when we
get paid over here.
