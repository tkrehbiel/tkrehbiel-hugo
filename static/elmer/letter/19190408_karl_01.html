[Karl, Elmer’s younger brother, is 10 at the time of this writing.]

April 8.
1919.

Dear Elmer;

I will write because there
is nothing to do. It is bed-
time now but I will righ
write. Mama and Paul and
I planted potatoes this
afternoon. I am going
fishing on Saturday.
We have two pigs that
have the rhumatysum [sic]
in the hind legs.
Rhiley tangled himself in
some wire and cut his leg bad.
Send Ted and me a plan for
a dugout and we can make
one.

Excuse all blots and
blunders, for when it
rains it sometimes
thunders.
