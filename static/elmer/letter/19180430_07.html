[A folded paper with two pages written book-style side-by-side.]

stand the towels. They look a whole lot
worse than Ma’s moprags and smell a
mile. I took one look and wiped my dishes
on my jumper. I wish you would send
me two or three towels; about 12 by 18” inches;
so that I can get them in my pockets.

This is a good bunch of fellows. There
are 60 in the room and only 3 or 4 look like they
got their start in life in barrooms. But even
they’re not bad. Our officer; we have only 1,
so far, seems to be alright too; we dont see
very much of him though. But we were lucky
with the corporals and sergeants that were placed
over us; they are all mighty good fellows. I
think I’m just as well off in the army as I
was out of it; I’ve got a whole lot off my
mind, certainly, I dont have to think out here.

About 10 minutes after I got settled here I
began to think of things that I forgot to bring;
shaving brush, soap, mirror, shoe oil, clothes
brush - they sewed us a pair of overalls and
a jumper, a blue denim uniform; they call it

[New page.]

here; mine is about kahki [sic] colored now from
dust. But I can buy all these things from
the post exchange; I did buy ’til my money
ran out. So I wanted you to see about my
check; I think I can get it cashed out here.
My full address is

Elmer H Krehbiel
Attached Co. H, 316th Inf.
Barracks T-37
Camp Meade

Someone take or telephone that down to
Mr. J.A. Chamberlain, Franklin School.
Phone Main 6000, that’s District of Columbia
switchboard, then branch 550 and ask for
Mr. Chamberlain. Or you can look him up in
the phone book and call him at his house. Give
him that address and he will have my check
sent out. I need it now.

I wish you would send me that roll
of paper that I brought home from school;
