“aller(?) au cinema” which means “take in
a movie” and visit places and people
of interest. After an early supper I leave
for Camp and get in between nine and
ten, tired but content and ready to begin
another weeks work. We bounce(?) out of
our downy bunks at 5:30, chow immediately
and commence operations at 7:00 A.M. - 12.00
to 1:00 is dinner and is enjoyed by all in-
cluding millions and millions of yellow-
jackets which are attached to our outfit.
They are so plentiful that each man has a
tribe of his own and the popular form of
address is “Good morning, how are your bees?”
They are very loving but jealous and do not
hesitate to stab you if you show any inclination
to flirt with bees of another family or tribe
or whatever it is bees have. They are little
but they sit down awfully hard.

I am afraid it is not possible for you to come
down here, although I wish you could; and
I suppose I will never get up there for it is
too close to where the War is. If you ever
get a chance to get with(?) us gobble it up

[The letter ends there. It feels like there might have been more but it is missing.]
