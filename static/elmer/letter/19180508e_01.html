[Undated. Estimated May 8-9, 1918.]

Camp Meade, Md.

Dear folks,

One thing about the army, nobody
knows anything about anything. In the evening we
hear tales of what we are going to do the next
day and then we know we’re going to do something
else. When we got our first typhoid shot the old men
told us we got our next one in ten days, now
said two weeks, some said we might get all three
at once. They said that if the left arm was still
sore, they’ed [sic] put the next shot in the right arm;
they said that they’ed [sic] put it into the left arm,
no matter how sore it was, and so on. Everyone
said different. But they agreed on two things; that
was that the second shot was given with a bicycle
pump and that it made you awfully sick.

We went out for drill as usual Monday
morning. I had quite an experience. I am
used to working my knife, fork and spoon
first, then sticking them down into the top of
my leggins [sic] while I washed my other dishes.
