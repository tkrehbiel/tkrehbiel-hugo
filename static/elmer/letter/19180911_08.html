We soon marched out of our landing(?)
place, marched 4 miles back parallel to
the railroad. There we first learned that we
were close to the front, for we were kept under
the trees as much as possible, and given an
alarm signal to warn of the approach of a
hostile aeroplane. We marched only about 5
hours, then camped in an open field, a cold
wet camp, as usual. Early in the morning
we had to break camp and(?) pitch [?] tents
under the trees. We stayed there till(?) it
was almost dark, then broke camp and
went into town, into billets. Perhaps you dont
know what billets are; I’ll give you a definition
that one of the boys sent home, “A billet is
a place where the cows and horses move out
and American soldiers move in.” All the
same, we were grateful for this barn, for it was
dry and warm. Also, we got cots from a French
YMCA nearby, so for the first time in three
months we slept off the ground.

We stayed there(?) only a night and day.
The town differed from everyone we’ve seen so
far in that we could buy things to eat.
