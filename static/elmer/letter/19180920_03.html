I used to often think of you kids in
our last camp. I bet you would have enjoyed
it there. It was a fine, big beech and oak
woods, the nicest place in the world to camp
out in. And the nuts were ripe and falling
fast; you could sit down in one spot and
pick half a pocketful without moving. I have
a sockful with me now and they are mighty
good things to chew on when you have nothing
to do but lay around. The woods we are in now
was also a fine place once but it is too shot
up now to be beautiful. And so many men
have tramped around that the ground is all
soft, squashy black mud. But such a dug-
out as I am living in would make a fine
camp. If you’re willing to do an awful lot
of work you can put one out in our woods and
then you can camp out summer and winter.
