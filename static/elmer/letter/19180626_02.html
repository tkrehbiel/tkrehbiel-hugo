Letter from Elmer, June 26, 1918 — Page 2

By that time it had begun
to rain; just a fine drizzle. It didn’t
look like much, but I thought I’d play
safe and ditch my tent before I crawled
in. So I got a good, deep ditch around,
crawled in, kicked off my shoes and
leggins [sic] and made a bed of my
blankets, as well as I could, then tried
to go to sleep. But I couldn’t.

Then along about 11 o’clock the
rain began to come down hard. I heard
fellows all around me getting up to
ditch their tents [–] and I had a good
laugh on them. But [–] it was only
for a minute; my tent began to leak
like a piece of mosquito netting. So
I had to squirm around for my poncho
and cover over with that. Then after
a while the wind began to rise, and
since the ground was soaked soft, the
tents began to go down right and left.
I knew mine would go sooner or later
but I hated to get out so I trusted to
luck and eventually went to sleep.

I awoke early in the morning.
