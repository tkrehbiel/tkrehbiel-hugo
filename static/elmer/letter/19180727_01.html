[A shockingly difficult letter to scan, due to the fact that it was written on 3 large sheets of paper, that were just too big for my scanner, and it was folded into a booklet style. Some portions were physically cut from the letter by the censor, which made it even more difficult to handle. I wrote little page numbers in the corner with red ink to ensure I got them in the right order, the only modification I made to any diary or letter.]

July 27, 1918

Dear folks.

You probably received the
card announcing that our ship had ar-
rived safely overseas. We are now in our bil-
lets and I am at last so settled that I can
write once again. It is only a week since I last
wrote you, but I’ve had experiences enough since
then to fill a whole encyclopedia. I’m starting
this letter with only three sheets of paper, so I’ll
have to send it as a continued story. I will be-
gin where I left off last and write it up day
by day, following the notes in my diary.

The day before we landed we
had a submarine scare. We had just stood
“Abandon Ship” drill and our fatigue detail had
gone down to clean the hold, when the alarm bells
rang. I was right up against the side of the ship.
