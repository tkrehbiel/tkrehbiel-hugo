Aug. 12, ’18.

Dear folks.

I am not getting mail very fast,
but if you are writing as regularly as I am,
there ought to be a bunch of it on the way
over. I can write now just as easily and
naturally as I breathe.

We are pretty well organized now,
and settled down. We devote our mornings, now
to intrenching [sic], laying out defences [sic], practise [sic] march
[–] and so on. In the afternoon we go through
regular(?) drills, mostly extended order and
battle formations. The most hated drill of all,
gas drill, we have the most often. We often
get a “Gas” alarm as we are marching along, then
we must march for half an hour or an hour
with our masks on. My mask always has a
little pool of sweat in the bottom.

This morning we had a pretty long
walk. We had some trenches to dig, and
the ground around here, rocky as it is, is
too valuable for such things, so we went
way off, to the top of a hill that just showed
on the skyline. Here the ground was poor enough
so we set to work. We layed [sic] out our trenches
