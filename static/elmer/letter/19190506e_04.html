I think its this country that
people have in mind when they speak
of beautiful France. As I have
said, Spring is here and the whole
countryside is green. The wheat, which
in Haute Marne was just showing
up is here two feet high. The vin-
yards [sic], which occupy three fourths of
this territory, are sprouting buds.
Alongside the road are immeasurable(?)
wild flowers. Three are familiar, daisy
dandelion and violet, but there are
dozens of others that I dont know at
all. Unfortunately the landscape is
sadly disfigured by the trees. Wood
is scarce, so they cut the tops off
trees about 10 feet from the ground
and every two or three years the
branches are lopped off. The result
is an ugly, knotty stump, with a
few ragged branches at the top, like
a few hairs on a bald man’s head.
