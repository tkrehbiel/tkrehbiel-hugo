The country we passed through was much the
same, only it was largely wooded. We passed
through villages, though, which must have where
American soldiers were evidently a new sight, for
the inhabitants showed a very lively curiosity.
When we halted the boys and girls gathered round
us, lifting our rifles, pulling out our bayonets,
[–] examining our packs and giving us a general
looking over. One fellow even put on a pack and
carried it a while to see how it felt. [Writing changes.] We tried
hard to buy something to eat at each halt, but
the country was like Mother Hubbard’s cupboard.
We did get water, though, for as soon as we
could make the people understand what we
wanted they all dashed inside and brought
us bucketfuls. The officers gave warning of
germs and so on, as usual, but we would
have drunk it if it had been full of
[–] catarpillars [sic].
