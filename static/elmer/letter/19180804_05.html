Letter from Elmer, August 4, 1918 — Page 5

I am now a corporal*, in charge of a squad
of automatic riflemen. The whole organization
of a company has been changed, and all the
57 varieties of deployments that we have been
learning will have to be forgotten. No rest for the
weary. If I’d known six months ago what I
know now, I would now be at the American
University on chemistry work. But as it is, the
only way out is by means of the hardest work.

[* Research note: Company H Morning Reports indicate Elmer was appointed a Corporal on August 3rd, effective August 1st.]

I have been well all along, but my feet are
troubling me a great deal. They are flat as pan-
cakes, you know, and the arches are aching day
and night. The drilling on that rough field, and
the hikes on the hard roads, are too much for them.
The right foot is swollen slightly. I have been
having the doctor bandage them up, but there has
been no(?) relief yet.

I have received no mail yet. A bundle came
in today but there was nothing for me so I’ll
have to give you a jarring. One month without
news from home is one of the horrors of war that
there is no excuse for. Remember the Literary Digest
and the movie magazine; I suppose you’ve already
found out that candy cant be sent. I have bestowed
on the postmaster a fervent curse, but I admit he
was wise, because otherwise about 5,000,000 pounds
of mail would be coming over every week.

There is plenty more to write about but I’ll stop
now. Notice the upper corner of the envelope, there
has been another change in the address.

Your
Elmer.
Censored
[CC?] Cunningham(?)
1st Lt 316 Inf

[If I’m reading the right envelope, Elmer’s new return address is:
[Corp] Elmer H. Krehbiel
[Co] H, 316th Inf, U.S.N.A.
[?] Am. E. F. redacted]

[Research note: U.S.N.A. = United States National Army.]
