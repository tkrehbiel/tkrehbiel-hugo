Also, Ted wants a German rifle and
maybe a machine gun. I could get him
the rifle right now, also empty shells, or
loaded ones too for that matter, cartridge
cases for cannon, long heavy brass cylinders, they are,
belts of bullets for machine guns, which make
a pretty docoration; we have some in our
dugout; or French bayonets or guns, in fact
anything he might want in that line, for
this woods is full of them. But as to the
machine gun, he can give up hopes. We had
a German machine gun shown to us, and
its operation explained, so we could use
it in case we captured it, and believe me
it is not a thing that I would pack
across the Atlantic. I think they weigh
about 300 pounds. But I would like to have
one of our Browning automatics after its
all over. They are a pretty little gun.

I cant think of anything that I
could send through the mail that would
make a good souvenier, or I would send it.
Maybe something will turn up later on.
