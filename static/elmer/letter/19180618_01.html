Camp Meade, June 18

[Note that the YMCA paper says 1917 but this was actually 1918.]

Dear Esther.

I want you to send that lithium
Carbonate that I got from Gilman’s and
that that [sic] I had there, up to the General
Electric Company. They are going to reduce
it for me. Get a mailing carton; one like
those that I’ve been getting from Einer(?) and
Amend(?), if you can, and put all the carbonate
together. You will find about a pound and a
half in two tin syrup cans on the back of
the shelves; it is labeled Lithium Carbonate.
If you cant get a mailing carton, fix it
up in a package so that it wont burst in
the mail. Send it special delivery. I’ll
be in on Saturday and pay you back.

Now do this as soon as you get
this note. If you havn’t [sic] got the money, pawn
your watch. I want it rushed; that’s why
I’m sending this letter special delivery.
They are going to make for me a chemical
that I’ve been trying to get for two years. (over)
