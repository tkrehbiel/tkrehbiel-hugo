Letter from Elmer, May 8, 1918 — Page 4

The fellows agreed that it was about a 5 1/2 mile
walk altogether. I said 4. The sergeant said
it wasn’t 3. I hardly felt it, except that
my shoe chafed me badly coming back.

We are in uniform now, and we dont look
a bit like a bunch of North East rookies. I got
a fine fit all around. All except my shoes. They
are big enough for me to take a bath in.
But they say that’s the way to have them,
your feet will spread when you get a heavy
pack on. They say every successive pair of shoes has
to be a size larger. You get a new pair every
3 months. Hence if the war lasts 3 years, I’ll
be wearing No. 19s.

All’s well with me. One of my newly
filled teeth has developed a bad ache and
it keeps me awake sometimes. But other-
wise I’m all right.

We are certainly lucky(?) in our officers
and non commissioned officers. Our lieu-
tenant, Lieutenant Johnson, is the best
fellow that ever was. He is an engineer who
has spent most of his life in Alaska. When
we’re out on hikes, or get a rest at drill he
mixes right in with us and tells stories,
or jokes or talks with us. The sergeants
are partly old army men and partly men

[Research note: Lieutenant “Alaska” Johnson was famously well-liked by the 316th. He was killed in action near Montfaucon.]
