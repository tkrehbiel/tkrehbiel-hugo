[This page from Elmer’s father is undated, but it may have been part of the batch of letters sent on March 9, because of the references to the much-discussed rifle, which Elmer first mentioned in February, and Elmer later replied about it in April.]

Dear Elmer, I will [?]
add something in the way of(?)
postscript to what Ma wrote
I think you had better make for
Montfaucon and get the rifle. You
are not more than twenty five miles
from there. Then make for Toulouse
as fast as you can. I hear that there
are about one thousand of our men
attending the university there, both
enlisted men and officers and you
could put(?) in some time there to
much better advantage than drill
or manouver [sic]. Did you ever
receive a money order I sent you
last August?

Will close for this time
Your loving father.

[FYI it looks like there’s writing on the opposite side, but it’s actually ink from where this page was folded. If you flip the page vertically, you can see the same lines from this page echoed again.]
