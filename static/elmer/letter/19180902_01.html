Sept. 2 1918

Dear folks.

I’m starting a letter, but
I dont know if I’ll finish it. We had
our usual drills all last week. Saturday
night we were all tired out, we crawled to
bed early. About 3 oclock the next morning
the bugles began blowing. We got outside, and
were told to get breakfast immediately, then
make up heavy packs for three days march.
At 4.30 we were off, out for a big divisional
maneuver. We hiked till about 9.20, covering
12 miles then we pitched tents in a little
field. We got dinner and lay around till
4 oclock, when an order suddenly came to
break camp and return to billets. We made
the march in 4 hours. We had covered ±4 miles
on our day of rest and believe me we were
tired.
