though, it is a mystery what becomes(?)
of all the water that falls here. It
rains almost continuously four months
of the year, and twice a week the other
8 months, and yet France has no rivers
that amount to anything. The Loire is
just about two miles from here and it
is only a slightly overgrown creek.

If it wasn’t for the everlasting
rain, life here would be one grand,
sweet, song(?). This is a huge hospital
base; there are 20 hospitals capable of
holding 40,000 patients. About half of
Co. H. is scattered around through them so
I can always go visiting. We only work
five hours a day, just enough to give us
an appetite. The food is good, by the exer-
cise of a little wit one can get plenty of it.
But, oh ye Gods, the MUD. I have ser-
iously thought of getting an empty medium(?)
bottle and sending you a sample of it.
