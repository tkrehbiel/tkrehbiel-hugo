We can get passes on Sundays to visit
within the regimental area so on Sunday I’m
going out of here and get off into the woods
somewhere. There is quite a forest to our
west(?); I mean one that covers quite an area,
I suppose the trees are small. I want to
see what a French forest looks like. There [–]
are little patches of pines scattered about the
fields, or rather field for there are no fences,
that(?) look(?) quite like home from a distance.
But when you get among them you find
that they are all planted in rows, the limbs
are uniformly trimmed and the ground is
clear of brush. It looks more like an orchard.

There does not seem to be any fruit(?)
around; the only thing I’ve seen is plums.
They have apple trees along the roads, some of them,
but the apples are only about the size of a walnut.
There are no grapes. So that takes this country
down several more notches in my esteem.

Elmer.

Censored
[CC Cunningham]
1st Lieut 316th Inf. N.A.
