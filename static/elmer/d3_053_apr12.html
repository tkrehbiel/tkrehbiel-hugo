Diary 3 — Apr 12

day of course. We had a
long walk, then drew up on(?)
a big, half wild field, where
the whole division was assem-
bled. It was a mighty im-
pressive sight. We stood
about till 10.30 then were
called to attention. General
Pershing galloped along our front
then began on the right and
inspected company by company.
We put our belts under our
slickers, so that we could
just drop them off when our
time came. Then we threw
them behind us, and stood
at our best attention, with our
bayonets fixed. The two ranks
were facing each other, the General
walked between them. He asked
us about our wounds etc. I
really did get to talk to him.
After the division had been
inspected our flags were
decorated. Then we passed
in review, a beautiful sight(?),
with our bayonets fixed.
