Diary 3 — May 28

2026_06_19_D3_Apr3 / D3_068_May28_IMG_0024.CR3

Soon a bank of woods became
visible on our left. These gave
way to farmed fields on both
sides, and then we knew
that we were once more in
the United States.

From Chester on up the
river on both sides were lined
with factories and all of them
opened their whistles wide
when we came along. The
railroads and steamers on
the rivers did the same.
About five miles below Phila(?)
a fleet of excursia(?) boats
met us and escorted us in.
When we reached the harbor
the whistles were so loud that
we could not hear our band
playing one hundred feet away.
We docked at the Snyder(?)
Ave whaff [sic] at Philadelphia, about
2 P.M.

loaded and, on the dock,
served ice cream and cake
by the Red Cross, the first
