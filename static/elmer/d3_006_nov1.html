woods. We had gotten the
vague information that our
battalion was in a woods, so
we left the road here and be-
gan to search for it. And while
looking for it, I got another little
cut from a shell fragment.

I was surprised at the
amount of food lying around.
I picked up a lot of cans and
made a good meal.

It was late in the afternoon
that I found the company. I
was hardly through shaking
hands when the lieutenant came
along. When he heard I was
he sent me right
back to the hospital.

I went to the first aid
station and had it dressed
then came back. But the
doctor said I had better
take a shot of A.T.S.(?), so
I walked back to a town,
Brabant, to the field dressing
station. I got the shot and
spent the night there.
