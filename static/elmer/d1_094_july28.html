Diary 1 — July 28

a 12 page letter. I only stopped

Sunday July 28

I thought the Sun-
days at Camp Meade
were bad, but they were not
half as bad as this. Here
there is absolutely no place
to go. I undressed and went
to sleep again all forenoon.

The afternoon brought
a gleam of joy. A bunch of
us went fishing in the canal.
On the way out we passed through
a fine rolling country. There were
no fences at all, the fields
just were continuous. The
soil was red, plentifully filled
with small rocks. The crops
were as a rule good, but
the oats were poor(?).

The country was beautiful.
