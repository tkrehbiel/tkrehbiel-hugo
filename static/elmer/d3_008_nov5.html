Tuesday Nov 5

Last night a raiding
party of 80 men from our
company went out after the Huns.
This morning 3 squads came back!
Some were lost among other companies,
others had stayed in the front
trenches, but by far the most of
the missing were on their way
to the hospital.

The raid was in conjunction
with a French outfit on our right.
The French always advance accord-
ing to schedule, the Americans,
according to the degree of resist-
ence. This happened to be not
serious, and our men went so
fast that the French were left
behind, and the right flank ex-
posed. The Germans got behind
our men with machine guns,
and they had to lay in shell
holes while there was machine-
gun fire from each side and
behind and shellfire in front.
The wonder is that any of them
came back.
