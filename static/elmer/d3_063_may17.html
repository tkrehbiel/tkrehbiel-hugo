Diary 3 — May 17

2026_06_19_D3_Apr3 / D3_063_May17_IMG_0014.CR3

We went to the delousing camp
where the whole regiment were put
through the delouser in about
two hours. And that delouser
was worthy of the name too, no
cootie or egg could live through
it.

We had dinner, then at
2 oclock packed up and marched
off. We went straight to the
dock where our ship, the Texan,
was waiting, and were promptly
loaded aboard. At 4 oclock
ship time (5 oclock French), we
cast off from the dock, turned
around in the narrow basin
and pulled out. We were soon
in deep water, although land
was visible on our right until
we turned in for the night.

Saturday May 17

We are now well
out to sea.

Some of the sailors said
last night that this was a
good boat in a calm. Another
added that she would roll
in drydock. This morning
there was a slight chop, none(?)
