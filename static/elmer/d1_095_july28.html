Diary 1 — July 28

2026_05_31_D1_July13 / D1_095_July28_IMG_0040.CR3

Then were gently sloping hills
all around, all well cultivated.
Here and there were patches of
wood. It looked much like
Virginia. It made me homesick.

The canal was pretty, and
like all France, it was well
kept. It was wider than
the Chesapeake and Ohio, and
I was surprised to find it
on the side of a hill. There were
fish in it; we saw them swim-
ming around, but none of them
bit.

We snatched some plums
from the trees. Those and onions(?)
were all there was in sight. We
came home along a road bor-
dered with English walnuts. I
guess this is where the nuts
that we pay ten cents a hand-
