Diary 3 — June 4

2026_06_19_D3_Apr3 / D3_070_June4_IMG_0028.CR3

Wednesday June 4

We turned in
our stuff today.
And believe me, if I had
known what it was like I
wouldnt have carried a pack
for the last six months.
They didn’t check anything,
in fact didn’t even see if
you were along. You just
dropped things in a pile
and went.

Thursday June 5

Today is the
day we have
been talking about for
a year. We are now out of
the army. We were shot
through the mill and given
our discharge. We must be
here Saturday though to
get our pay.

By the way, its just
two years ago today that
we(?) registered.

Friday June 6

Nothing again.
It rained today,
the first time since we’ve
been here. How different from
France.

[That was the final diary entry. The rest of the pages in the third diary are blank, except for miscellaneous writings on the final pages. It is not known if Elmer stayed another day at Camp Dix for his paycheck, or how/when he made his way home to Virginia.]
