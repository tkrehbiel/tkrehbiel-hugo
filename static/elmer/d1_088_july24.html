Diary 1 — July 24

2026_05_31_D1_July13 / D1_088_July24_IMG_0033.CR3

I moved that. So I
have a place that I
wouldn’t trade for any
in the billet.

The village is, of
course, all built of
stone. The stone here
is a whitish sort of
granite, which splits in
thin slabs. The houses
are all built of this
stone; even the roof is
of the same thing. The
walls are about 18” thick,
and the roofs are p??d [1:piled?]
the same. The rafters
are big, square hewn
trees. The roofs are very
high and steep.

The country is
rolling and hilly.
