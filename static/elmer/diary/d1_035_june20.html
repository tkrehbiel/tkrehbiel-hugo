In a World War I training context, cement grenades were exactly what they sound like: practice hand grenades made from cement (concrete), used to train soldiers in throwing and grenade handling without wasting live explosives.

A typical training grenade consisted of:

  • a metal handle or body similar in shape to a live grenade
  • a cement-filled head to approximate the weight
  • sometimes a small spotting charge or blank cartridge to indicate where it landed

The goal was to teach:

  • proper throwing technique
  • judging distance
  • use of cover
  • trench grenade tactics

without the danger and expense of using live grenades every day.

[That’s not a misread, it really says “not!” at the end there. Apparently that sarcastic sentence construction we associate with Wayne’s World goes back to at least 1918.]

Thursday June 20

We had battle pra-
ctise [sic] again this
morning, this time by the
whole regiment. I suppose
it went off all right, we
went through the whole
program. But our part
was of course small, so
I don’t know much of the
business.

In the afternoon
our winter clothing was
issued. Otherwise we were
on the assault course.

Friday June 21

today. They are as
rotten as we are.
