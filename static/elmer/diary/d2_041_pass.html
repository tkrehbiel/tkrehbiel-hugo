Vaux Pass

[A pass is written sideways on this page of the diary, in what looks like pen. Bold move to hand one’s diary to a lieutenant, when they allegedly weren’t supposed to be keeping diaries.]

Co H, 316 Inf
3rd Sept 1918
Pass Cpl Krehbiel to Vaux+return, between the
hours of 7 PM + 8.30 PM. this date only

[Signature] [1:? ? Cunningham?]
[?] [1:1st Lt] 316 Inf

[Research note: The mystery 1st Lieutenant Claude C. Cunningham was found on the outgoing Company H Transport Passenger Manifest list. He was later promoted to Captain, and reported wounded in action on November 14th (wound date unknown). He was administratively “dropped” from Company H on November 21st. He was not on the incoming passenger list in 1919. His fate is unknown.]

[The diary continues.]
