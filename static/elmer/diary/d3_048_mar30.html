We wound up about 12.30(?)
in a little town whose name
I never heard. The billet we
were assigned to is the worst
hole I ever saw in my life.
So as soon as we had(?) our
pack off we went off scouting
for La Chanbre(?). The best we
could do was a little loft.
So four of us went in there.
It was over a house where
two old people were, a man,
70, he told us, and a woman,
his daughter, I suppose.
We spent most of our time(?) in(?)
their kitchen. It was a little
room, furnished in oak(?), all
beautifully darkened with age.
There was an open fireplace
on which all the cooking was
done. The back of the fire-
place was a piece of cast
iron, set flush with the
wall. The chimney projected
out over it. To boil anything,
they hung the pot on a(?) hook(?)
which hung(?) down the chimney.

[“La chambre” is French for a room.]

[Research note: The Company H Morning Reports note the town as Ménil-sur-Saulx, 16 kilos from Longeville.]
