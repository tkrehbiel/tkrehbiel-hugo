[Yet another hard-to-read page, due to faded pencil and reflective light.]

We had hot meals, o??er
and supper from our rolling(?)
kitchen; I slept that night
in a hayloft.

Saturday Mar 28

Reveille the same
as yesterday. After
breakfast, I made some im-
provements in my pack.
Our overcoats and one blanket
were carried on the combat train.
I stuffed all that I could
into my overcoat pockets, and
put my extra shoes into the
sleeves. The overcoat weighed
a ton.

We started after breakfast
in a fine, gently falling snow.
After we got out from among
the buildings we found that
there was a stiff wind
from the south, driving the
snow almost horizontally.
It blew in our faces, which
we considered very tough(?).

But it kept on snowing,
with variations(?) of hard-
driven(?), stinging(?) sleet(?).
All the water was conducted(?)
