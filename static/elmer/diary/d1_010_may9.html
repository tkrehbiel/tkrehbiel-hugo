May 9 Thursday

Thursday regular
routine drill. Were
given 12 general orders to
learn. Were drilled in squads
for the first time. We had
a fine corporal; Leimbach by name.
Afternoon we had another
lecture on personal hygene [sic].
Drill from 2.00 till 4.30.

Went to the Liberty
Theater in the evening. A
great show, “Nothing But The Truth.”

Given the date (1918) and the title, I think it’s much more likely it’s a live stage comedy than a movie.

The key clue is that “Nothing But the Truth” was a hugely popular Broadway comedy by James Montgomery that opened in 1916 and toured extensively during the war years. It was based on a 1914 novel and was one of the big comedy hits of the era. (ESAT)

The plot

The premise is surprisingly modern:

A stockbroker named Robert Bennett is known for stretching the truth. During an argument, he accepts a wager that he can tell nothing but the truth for 24 hours.

As you might imagine, this immediately causes chaos:

  • He tells friends what he really thinks of them.
  • He reveals secrets people wish he’d keep quiet.
  • Social situations become disasters.
  • His business dealings become complicated.
  • His romance is threatened.

The humor comes from watching a normally polite society collide with brutal honesty. (Wikipedia)
