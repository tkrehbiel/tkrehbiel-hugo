Nothing was in sight
at dawn. But about 8 o’clock
our convoy made its appear-
ance; there were 8 destroyers,
very long and low in the
water. They rode beautifully.

The day passed as usual
till just after we formed for
mess. We had just gotten
down into the hold to
begin our cleaning up when
the alarm bell began to ring.
We grabbed our packs and
hurried on deck, just in
time to see the rearmost
destroyer heave a depth
bomb over. A submarine
had been sighted, the
French boat fired first, then
the America took it up.
