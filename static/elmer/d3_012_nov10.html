Diary 3 — Nov 10

2026_06_06_D3_Oct25 / D3_012_Nov10_IMG_0016.CR3

8 we were told the train was
ready. But when I got up off my
bench I found that I could hardly
stand. I managed to get to the
train, though, and then wrapped
up in a blanket and layed [sic] down
in the aisle.

Sunday Nov 10

The train travelled [sic] all
day and all night. And
I kept feeling worse and worse.
By morning I could not sit up
in the seat, so they put me on
a stretcher! By that time I
had a high fever, and all my joints
were swollen and aching. I couldn’t
move, not even turn over.

Late in the evening the
train stopped. But I couldn’t
get out; I just lay on my stretcher.
Soon(?) an(?) ambulance came along
and I was loaded into it.

That drive was the worst
kind of torture. The roads were
awful and the bumps about
threw me out of my litter.
But it ended at last. They
carried me into a ward and
rolled me out onto a bed.
