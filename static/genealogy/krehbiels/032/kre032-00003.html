This picture allegedly shows Centreville during the Civil War. It is also noteworthy because of the "Krehbiel's Korner" stamps on the back. (Note that the print was actually larger than my scanner, so this picture is cropped by an inch or so on the top and left.) Another photograph from moments earlier can be found on Wikipedia (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image:Centreville_1862.jpg), where the scene is described as "Main street and church guarded by Union soldiers." That photo is credited to George N. Barnard and James F. Gibson, dated May 1862.
